Price Action Dictionary

Essential concepts every trader must recognize instantly on the chart.

Order Blocks (OB)

Specific price zones where institutional players have piled up large orders. Often acts as a magnet for price to return and mitigate before continuing the trend.

Break of Structure (BOS)

Confirmed when a candle body closes above a previous high (bullish) or below a previous low (bearish), signaling trend continuation.

Liquidity Sweeps

When price briefly pokes beyond an obvious support/resistance level to trigger stop-losses (creating liquidity) before reversing direction aggressively.

Imbalance (FVG)

Fair Value Gaps are inefficiencies left in the market after a violent move. Price often returns to these gaps to "fill" them and restore equilibrium.

Change of Character (CHoCH)

The first sign of a potential reversal, where the price breaks the most recent significant structure point against the prevailing trend.

Premium vs Discount

Buying low (Discount) and selling high (Premium). Traders use Fibonacci tools (0.5 level) to determine if price is in a favorable zone for entry.

The Holy Grail is
Risk Management

You can be wrong 50% of the time and still be profitable. The secret isn't predicting the future; it's managing the downside when you are wrong and maximizing the upside when you are right.

The 1% Rule

Never risk more than 1% of your total equity on a single trade. This ensures you can survive a losing streak without blowing your account.

Risk to Reward (R:R)

Always aim for at least 1:2 or 1:3. If you risk $100, your target should be $200 or $300. This mathematical edge is superior to win rate.

Correlation Awareness

Don't open max risk positions on EURUSD, GBPUSD, and AUDUSD simultaneously. If the dollar moves, all your trades move together.
